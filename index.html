Bio

I am a third-year Computer Science student with a passion for artificial intelligence, machine learning, data science, and game development. My academic and project experience center on exploring the connections between computing and human well-being through intelligent, purposeful design.

I have developed several projects that reflect my interests in AI and simulation, including a Non-Preemptive Priority Scheduling Algorithm Simulator, The Serenity Institute—a mental healthcare facility simulation game—and multiple neural network models such as a Feedforward Neural Network and a Convolutional Neural Network for plastic bottle classification and weight prediction. These projects represent my growing expertise in applying computational intelligence to creative and socially meaningful contexts.

My long-term goal is to develop games that raise mental health awareness and to contribute to computing research at the intersection of artificial intelligence, interactive systems, and mental health. I am highly systematic, a critical thinker, and an aspiring computer scientist dedicated to advancing the role of technology in understanding and improving the human experience.
